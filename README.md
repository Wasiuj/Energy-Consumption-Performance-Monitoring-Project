# Energy-Consumption-Performance-Monitoring-Project
Executive Summary
The Energy Performance Dashboard shows the overall energy consumption trend across multiple sites from 2019 to 2024. Total electricity consumption reached 4.1 million kWh, with 1.0 million units of gas usage recorded within the same period. The average Energy Use Intensity (EUI) stands at 25.14, with a 20.43% year-on-year increase in kWh consumption, indicating rising energy demand across sites.
Although 56.25% of sites are using solar energy, a significant 44% of locations still rely entirely on non-solar energy, contributing to higher energy intensity and peak demand levels. The data highlights opportunities for solar expansion, load optimization, and targeted efficiency improvements across high-consumption sites.

Insights
1.	Warehouses and factories account for the majority of energy use, indicating operational loads as primary drivers.
2.	Nearly half of the sites still depend on non-solar energy, representing a major cost and sustainability opportunity.
3.	High-consumption sites are concentrated in a few locations, suggesting targeted interventions could yield significant savings.
4.	Energy demand is rising year-on-year, which may be due to production growth, equipment inefficiency, or lack of energy control measures.
5.	Peak demand fluctuations indicate inconsistent load management and potential inefficiencies in scheduling or equipment usage.

Recommendations
1.	Expand Solar Installations
o	Prioritize the 44% of non-solar sites.
o	Focus on top-consuming locations first for faster ROI.
2.	Target High-EUI Sites
o	Conduct energy audits at sites flagged with high EUI.
o	Replace inefficient equipment and improve insulation.
3.	Peak Load Management
o	Implement demand-side management strategies.
o	Shift non-critical operations to off-peak hours.
4.	Energy Efficiency in Warehouses and Factories
o	Upgrade lighting to LED.
o	Install energy-efficient motors and drives.
o	Introduce automated shutdown controls.
5.	Continuous Monitoring and KPI Tracking
o	Set energy reduction targets per site.
o	Monitor EUI and peak demand monthly.
________________________________________
Expected Outcomes
If the recommendations are implemented:
1.	10–20% reduction in total electricity consumption within 12–18 months.
2.	Lower peak demand charges through improved load management.
3.	Increased solar contribution to over 70% of sites.
4.	Reduction in EUI across high-consumption locations.
5.	Improved sustainability performance and lower carbon footprint.
6.	Operational cost savings and better energy budget control.
